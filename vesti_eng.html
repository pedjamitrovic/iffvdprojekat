Istvan Szabo - Winner of the "Belgrade Winner" award

Admin 02.07.2018.

Istvan Sabo, cult Hungarian director, is this year's winner of the IFF's "Belgrade Winner" award for outstanding contribution to film art. The key figure of the Hungarian film, Sabo began his career with very noticeable autobiographical films of the 1960s and 1970s, in order to make a real breakthrough on the international film scene of the 1980s, enlisting in history as the first (and almost only) Hungarian Oscar award winner.

He was born on February 18, 1938 in Budapest, where he studied at the prestigious Academy of Theater and Film Arts. At age 26 he directed his first feature film, "Epoch of Illusion" (Almodozasok Kora, 1964), which won him the Best Debut Award at the Festival in Locarno. His first films were partly autobiographical - "Father" (Apa, 1966), "Lovefilm" (Szerelmesfilm, 1970) and "Street Firefighters 25" (Tuzoltó utca 25, 1973) dealt with the experiences of his generation and recent Hungarian history.

In addition to the films, Sabo also testified on television, and he also directed several operas. He was one of the founders of the European Film Academy, and a professor at several world universities, including the Faculty of Dramatic Arts in Belgrade. He was awarded the Alexander Lifka Prize for outstanding contribution to film art at the 2007 Pali Film Festival.

46th IFF

Admin 17.06.2018.

Today in the City Assembly, the first press conference was held on the occasion of the forthcoming 46th IFF, where Ivona Jevtic, Natasa Ilic, Jugoslav Pantelic, Mladen Djordjevic and Marija Boskovic spoke.

Ivona Jevtic, the City Secretary for Culture and the President of the IFF Committee, was pleased to announce that the city of Belgrade has allocated a record sum of 30 million dinars for this year's IFF, which is expected as one of the most important city events. She highlighted a novel, projections at the Fontana cinema, aiming to bring the festival closer to the audience in all parts of the city.

Natasa Ilic, ed. director of the CEBEF, said that the Belgrade Festival Center has been organizing the IFF for the fourth year in a row and that it hopes that trust in this organization is now justified. She also added that culture is also in the heart of interest, which is shown by the support of the IFF by the Cabinet of the Prime Minister, mayors, the Secretariat for Culture and numerous public enterprises.

Jugoslav Pantelic, artistic director of the IFF, singled out some of the titles that should not be missed like Luke Gwadanjina, The Phantom Thread of Paul Thomas Anderson, Niotkuda Fatih Akin, You were never here Lin Remzi, Jupiter the month of Kornela Mundruca, the forgiveness of Roland Joffy, the Legend of the concubine of Chen Kajge. Some of the titles will only be revealed and get cult status, such as Vernost Mihailo Roska's film, and some films will have the audience in Belgrade to see among the first in the world, such as Eva Benoa Zakoa, and Ms. Malgorzata Sumovska, who come right from Berlin, and the latter and closes 46th IFF.

Mobile Telephony of Serbia has been sponsoring the IFF for ten years, and Maria Bošković wished for every successful festival on this behalf and stated that it is a great pleasure for them to support the manifestation that represents our capital as a city of culture, which it actually is.
